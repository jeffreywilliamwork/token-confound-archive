### **1. Newtonian Escape Velocity Derivation**
The escape velocity \( v \) for a mass \( m \) to escape a gravitational body of mass \( M \) and radius \( r \) is derived by equating kinetic energy to gravitational potential energy:
\[
\frac{1}{2}mv^2 = \frac{GMm}{r}.
\]
Solving for \( v \):
\[
v = \sqrt{\frac{2GM}{r}}.
\]
Setting \( v = c \) (the speed of light) gives the Schwarzschild radius:
\[
r_s = \frac{2GM}{c^2}.
\]
