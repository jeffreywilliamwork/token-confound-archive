**Derivation of the Navier-Stokes Equations**

**1. Conservation of Mass (Continuity Equation):**
The continuity equation enforces mass conservation. Consider a fluid element with density \(\rho\) and velocity \(\mathbf{u}\). The rate of change of mass within a fixed volume must equal the net mass flux through its boundaries:
\[
\frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{u}) = 0.
\]
- **Physical Meaning**: The first term (\(\partial \rho/\partial t\)) is the local density change. The second (\(\nabla \cdot (\rho \mathbf{u})\)) represents mass flux
