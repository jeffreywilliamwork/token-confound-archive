The processing of eukaryotic mRNA from transcription to a translation-ready state involves several critical steps, each mediated by specific molecular machinery. Here's a detailed breakdown:

### 1. **5' Capping with 7-Methylguanosine**
   - **Initiation**: Begins during transcription by RNA polymerase II (RNA Pol II). The phosphorylated C-terminal domain (CTD) of RNA Pol II recruits capping enzymes.
   - **Steps**:
     1. **RNA Triphosphatase** removes one phosphate from the 5' triphosphate of the nascent RNA.
     2. **Guanylyltransferase** adds a GTP in reverse orientation, forming a 5'-5' triphosphate bond.
     3. **Methyltransferase** transfers a methyl group from S-adenosylmethionine to the N7 position of guanine, creating the **7-methylguanosine cap**.
   - **Function**: Protects mRNA from exonucleases and aids in ribosome recognition during translation.

### 2. **Splicing by the Spliceosome**
   - **Spliceosome Composition**: A dynamic complex of snRNPs (U1, U2, U4, U5, U6) and auxiliary proteins (e.g., SF1, U2AF).
   - **Key Steps**:
     1. **Recognition**: U1 binds the 5' splice site (GU), U2 recognizes the branch site (adenine), and U2AF binds the 3' splice site (AG).
     2. **Assembly**: The tri-snRNP (U4/U6/U5) joins, displacing U1 and U4, forming the active spliceosome.
     3. **Transesterification Reactions**:
        - **First**: The branch site adenine's 2' OH attacks the 5' splice site, cleaving the RNA and forming a lariat intermediate.
        - **Second**: The 3' OH of the freed exon attacks the 3' splice site, ligating the exons and releasing the intron as a lariat.
   - **Outcome**: Introns are excised, exons joined, and the spliceosome disassembles.

### 3. **3' Cleavage and Polyadenylation**
   - **Signals**: AAUAAA (poly-A signal) and a downstream GU-rich element.
   - **Machinery**:
     - **CPSF** binds AAUAAA.
     - **CstF** interacts with the GU-rich region.
     - **Cleavage Factor** cuts the RNA 10–30 nucleotides downstream of AAUAAA.
     - **Poly-A Polymerase** adds 200–250 adenines using ATP, templated by the RNA signal.
   - **Stabilization**: **Poly-A Binding Proteins (PABPs)** coat the tail, enhancing mRNA stability and translation.

### 4. **Nuclear Export via the NPC**
   - **Packaging**: mRNA forms an **mRNP** complex with proteins like TREX, CBC (cap-binding complex), and PABPs.
   - **Quality Control**: Proper capping, splicing, and polyadenylation are verified before export.
   - **Mechanism**:
     - **TREX Complex** links transcription/processing to export.
     - **Export Receptors** (e.g., CRM1) recognize CBC and PABPs, guiding the mRNP through the **Nuclear Pore Complex (NPC)**.
     - **Ran GTPase** provides directionality by hydrolyzing GTP to GDP.
   - **Outcome**: Mature mRNA enters the cytoplasm for translation.
